DiVeQ-PQ on MNIST — Experiment Report 1

Date: 2026-04-27  |  Experiments: exp1–exp29  |  Running now: exp30 (interleaved G=16), exp31 (interleaved G=8)

Architecture

Encoder: 784 → 512 → 512 → LATENT_DIM  (GELU)
DiVeQ-PQ: G groups × K codewords each
Decoder: LATENT_DIM → 512 → 512 → 784  (sigmoid)

DiVeQ: zq = z + ‖c*−z‖₂ · sg[normalize(v + d̃)]
Gradient flows through scalar magnitude only — no STE, no commitment loss.

Pareto Front: Bits vs MSE

DiVeQ vs STE Comparison

Key finding: At 200 epochs, DiVeQ (0.007041) ≈ STE (0.007000). At 500 epochs, DiVeQ improves to 0.006913 while STE degrades to 0.007577. The commitment loss increasingly conflicts with reconstruction loss in late training.

Training Curves (Best LATENT=16 Experiments)

Lazy Groups Problem (LATENT=32)

exp28 (LATENT=32, G=16, D_g=2, 500ep, contiguous slicing): 6 of 16 groups have <100/512 codewords used. Groups 0,1 (dims 0-3) and groups 9,10,13 (dims 18-21, 26-27) carry little encoder variance. Final MSE 0.004223 — still 16.7% above the LATENT=32 AE ceiling (0.003618).

Fix being tested (exp30/31): Interleaved grouping — group g gets dims g, g+G, g+2G, ... — mixes high/low variance dimensions within each group.
